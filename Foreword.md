# Foreword

In 2016, I became one of the first researchers in crypto. It meant basically nothing at the time because the role barely existed yet. In fact, I think the only reason I stood out was the lack of competition, a stark contrast to today's reality, where the brightest minds compete for only a handful of positions.

Back then, there were no established research methodologies, no institutional frameworks, and certainly no clear path for others to follow. When I joined The Block in 2018 as one of the company's first employees, I had the opportunity to build a trustworthy media company supported by research and data insights. It's important to remember that at the time, it wasn't even clear if the industry would survive.

Bloomberg, Financial Times, and Wall Street Journal were relentlessly critical. Journalists seemed to almost enjoy the fact that Bitcoin was trading below $4,000 for several months despite reaching $20,000 in 2017. There wasn't even a hint of the institutional interest we're accustomed to today.

Despite that uncertainty, The Block began building what would eventually become the industry's first institutional research arm from the ground up. Over the following years, we scaled to a team of more than 40 researchers and evolved into the go-to media company for institutional readers seeking serious crypto analysis.

Throughout that journey, I faced a persistent challenge that ultimately inspired this book: **how do you quickly bring talented people up to speed in an industry that moves this fast?** Crypto is perhaps the hardest industry to master because of the sheer volume of bad and biased information, largely due to vastly different financial interests at play.

Over the years, we received more than 10,000 applications for research positions. We looked for candidates with insatiable intellectual curiosity and a near-obsessive desire to learn, but we also needed people who already possessed a solid baseline of knowledge. **The problem was simple: there was no single resource to recommend.**

Don't get me wrong. Excellent resources exist. Andreas M. Antonopoulos's *Mastering Bitcoin* and *Mastering Ethereum* are essential reading for anyone who wants to truly master both protocols. But they're protocol-specific and not frequently updated. YouTube and X overflow with promotional content. The truly high-quality research is locked behind paywalls or scattered across hundreds of sources. Academic papers are too theoretical. Podcasts assume too much prior knowledge. Technical documentation is comprehensive but intimidating.

Every resource I could point someone toward had significant limitations, whether in accessibility, completeness, objectivity, or approachability.

**This book exists because I wish someone else had written it first, but nobody did.** After stepping down as CEO of The Block, I realized I finally had the time to tackle this project and that it could serve as a genuine contribution to the industry. It's the resource I would have given to every promising candidate who applied to our research team. It's the foundation I wish I'd had when I started.

What makes this book different isn't just its comprehensiveness. At over 80,000 words covering everything from Bitcoin's UTXO model to quantum-resistant cryptography, it certainly is thorough. **The real difference is the approach.** This book was written for people who want to actually understand how this technology works, not just invest in it. Every chapter builds on the previous ones, creating a coherent narrative rather than a scattered collection of facts. Technical concepts are explained with both precision and clarity, assuming intellectual curiosity but not prior expertise. **Each of the 15 chapters has been reviewed by an expert in that specific field,** ensuring technical accuracy and real-world relevance.

I've made this book completely open source and freely available. You can download it, read it, share it, and even contribute feedback to improve it. This reflects both practical and philosophical considerations. Practically, the industry moves too fast for traditional publishing. By the time a printed crypto book hits shelves, portions are already outdated. Open source allows continuous updates as protocols evolve and new innovations emerge. Philosophically, basic education shouldn't be gatekept. We need more people who genuinely understand how this industry works, and paywalls only limit that understanding to those who can afford it.

Across 15 chapters spanning the entire crypto landscape, this book will give you a complete understanding of the industry from technology to market structure to emerging innovations. You'll understand why Bitcoin's proof-of-work creates security through energy expenditure, how Ethereum's rollup-centric roadmap trades composability for scalability, how stablecoins grew to threaten traditional payment rails, and how seriously we should take the quantum threat.

**The industry doesn't need more hype or more dismissive skepticism. It needs more people who can think critically** about what this technology actually does, where it creates genuine value, and where it falls short of its promises. Whether you're considering a career in crypto or simply trying to understand what all the noise is about, this book provides the foundation you need.

Those who read it and retain the knowledge will know more than 99% of people working in crypto full-time. Trust me.

**Larry Cermak**
